# Direct Vasodilators

## Examples

- `hydralazine`
- `minoxidil`
- `diazoxide`
- `sodium nitroprusside`

> These act directly on vascular smooth muscle to cause relaxation, reducing peripheral resistance and BP.

## Mechanism of Action (MOA)

| Drug                       | Mechanism                                                                                                                              |
| -------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **`hydralazine`**          | Direct relaxation of arteriolar smooth muscle (via ↑ cGMP, unclear exact mechanism). Preferentially acts on **arterioles**, not veins. |
| **`minoxidil`**            | Opens **ATP-sensitive K⁺ channels** → hyperpolarization of vascular smooth muscle → arteriolar vasodilation.                           |
| **`diazoxide`**            | Similar to minoxidil — opens K⁺ channels → relaxation of arterioles (also inhibits insulin release).                                   |
| **`sodium nitroprusside`** | Releases **nitric oxide (NO)** → activates guanylate cyclase → ↑ cGMP → relaxation of both **arteries and veins**.                     |

## Pharmacodynamics

- ↓ **Peripheral vascular resistance** → ↓ afterload.
- **Reflex tachycardia and fluid retention** often occur — must be used with β-blocker and diuretic.
- `hydralazine` → primarily arteriolar dilator (reduces afterload).
- `nitroprusside` → balanced dilator (reduces preload + afterload); potent and rapid-acting.
- `minoxidil` → very powerful arteriolar dilator; stimulates hair growth.
- `diazoxide` → also used for hypertensive emergencies and hypoglycemia due to insulin inhibition.

## Pharmacokinetics

| Drug                   | Route    | Onset                        | Duration | Remarks                                                                      |
| ---------------------- | -------- | ---------------------------- | -------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| `hydralazine`          | Oral, IV | 20–30 min (oral), 5 min (IV) | 6–12 h   | Metabolized by acetylation; “slow acetylators” → ↑ toxicity                  |
| `minoxidil`            | Oral     | Slow                         | Long     | Prodrug; hepatic conversion to active metabolite                             |
| `diazoxide`            | IV       | 1–5 min                      | 6–18 h   | Long acting; used only IV                                                    |
| `sodium nitroprusside` | IV       | Seconds                      | 1–2 min  | Ultra-short acting; decomposes in light; forms cyanide → requires monitoring |

## Indications

- **Severe / resistant hypertension** (`hydralazine`, `minoxidil`)
- **Hypertensive emergencies** (`nitroprusside`, `diazoxide`)
- **Pregnancy-induced hypertension** (`hydralazine`)
- **Heart failure (with nitrates)** — `hydralazine + isosorbide dinitrate`
- **Alopecia (topical minoxidil)**

## Adverse Effects

- Reflex **tachycardia**, **palpitations**
- **Fluid retention**, edema (due to renin activation)
- **Headache**, flushing, nausea
- **Hydralazine:** lupus-like syndrome (esp. slow acetylators), peripheral neuropathy
- **Minoxidil:** hypertrichosis (↑ hair growth), pericardial effusion
- **Diazoxide:** hyperglycemia (↓ insulin release)
- **Nitroprusside:** cyanide toxicity, metabolic acidosis, hypotension

## Cautions

- Always combine with **β-blocker + diuretic** (to prevent reflex effects).
- Avoid prolonged use of `nitroprusside` → risk of cyanide accumulation.
- Avoid in ischemic heart disease (reflex tachycardia ↑ O₂ demand).
- Dose adjustment in renal/hepatic impairment (esp. `hydralazine`).

## Contraindications

- **Tachycardia**, **angina**, or **heart failure** (uncontrolled)
- **Aortic dissection** (avoid rapid BP drop)
- **Porphyria** (`hydralazine`)
- **Pregnancy** (`minoxidil`, `nitroprusside`)

## Key Points Summary

- Direct vasodilators act **on smooth muscle**, **not via autonomic receptors**.
- Always combine with **β-blocker + diuretic**.
- **Reflex responses (↑HR, ↑renin)** limit their use alone. |
