# Calcium Channel Blockers

## Comparison

| Feature                                | Dihydropyridines (DHPs)                                                              | Non-Dihydropyridines (non-DHPs)                                    |
| :------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Examples**                           | `nifedipine`, `amlodipine`, `felodipine`, `nicardipine`, `clevidipine`, `nimodipine` | `verapamil`, `diltiazem`                                           |
| **Primary Site of Action**             | **Vascular smooth muscle** → strong **arterial vasodilation**                        | **Heart + vessels** → ↓ SA & AV nodal conduction, ↓ contractility  |
| **Effect on Heart Rate**               | Reflex **↑ HR** (tachycardia)                                                        | **↓ HR** (bradycardia)                                             |
| **Effect on Myocardial Contractility** | Minimal direct effect                                                                | **Negative inotropy**                                              |
| **Effect on AV Conduction**            | No significant effect                                                                | **Depresses AV conduction**                                        |
| **Blood Pressure Effect**              | ↓ Peripheral resistance → ↓ BP                                                       | ↓ BP (less potent vasodilation) + ↓ CO                             |
| **Use in Angina**                      | Chronic stable & variant angina                                                      | Chronic stable, variant, and **supraventricular tachyarrhythmias** |
| **Use in Hypertension**                | Commonly used (esp. `amlodipine`)                                                    | Used if patient also has arrhythmia                                |
| **Use in Heart Failure**               | Avoid (may cause reflex sympathetic activation)                                      | Avoid (`verapamil` especially) — negative inotropy                 |
| **Adverse Effects**                    | Flushing, headache, edema, reflex tachycardia, gingival hyperplasia                  | Bradycardia, AV block, constipation (`verapamil`), ↓ contractility |
| **Contraindications**                  | Unstable angina, hypotension                                                         | Heart block, bradycardia, LV dysfunction                           |
| **Metabolism**                         | Hepatic (CYP3A4)                                                                     | Hepatic (CYP3A4)                                                   |
| **Distinctive Drugs**                  | `Nimodipine` (cerebral vasospasm), `Clevidipine` (IV for HT emergency)               | `Verapamil` (cardiac-selective), `Diltiazem` (intermediate)        |
