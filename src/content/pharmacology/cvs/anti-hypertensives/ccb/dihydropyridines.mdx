# Dihydropyridines

## Examples

- `nifedipine`
- `amlodipine`
- `felodipine`
- `nicardipine`
- `nimodipine`
- `lacidipine`
- `isradipine`

> Act mainly on vascular smooth muscle → potent vasodilation → ↓ peripheral resistance and BP.

## Mechanism of Action (MOA)

- Block **L-type voltage-gated Ca²⁺ channels** in vascular smooth muscle → ↓ Ca²⁺ influx → **vasodilation**, mainly arteriolar.
- Little or **no direct cardiac depressant effect** (unlike non-dihydropyridines).
- ↓ **Afterload** → ↓ BP.
- Cause reflex **sympathetic activation** → may increase HR slightly (esp. short-acting ones like `nifedipine`).

## Pharmacodynamics

- **Vascular:** Potent arteriolar vasodilation → ↓ total peripheral resistance, ↓ afterload.
- **Heart:** Minimal direct effect on conduction or contractility.
- **Coronary circulation:** Improve coronary blood flow → useful in angina.
- **Cerebral arteries:** `nimodipine` preferentially dilates cerebral vessels (used in subarachnoid hemorrhage).

## Pharmacokinetics

| Drug          | Route    | Onset         | Duration      | Remarks                                                          |
| ------------- | -------- | ------------- | ------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| `nifedipine`  | Oral     | 20–30 min     | 4–6 h (short) | May cause reflex tachycardia; avoid short-acting in hypertension |
| `amlodipine`  | Oral     | Slow          | 36–48 h       | Long-acting; once daily; minimal reflex tachycardia              |
| `felodipine`  | Oral     | 15–30 min     | 8–12 h        | Intermediate duration                                            |
| `nicardipine` | Oral, IV | 5–10 min (IV) | 8 h           | Used in hypertensive emergencies (IV)                            |
| `nimodipine`  | Oral, IV | 30 min        | 8 h           | Lipophilic; crosses BBB; used in cerebral vasospasm              |

## Indications

- **Hypertension** (especially in elderly & isolated systolic hypertension)
- **Angina pectoris[^1]** (chronic stable, Prinzmetal’s/variant)
- **Hypertensive emergencies[^2]** (`nicardipine`, `clevidipine`)
- **Subarachnoid hemorrhage** (`nimodipine`)
- **Raynaud’s phenomenon** (off-label)

[^1]: **Angina pectoris**: A symptom complex caused by transient myocardial ischaemia, which occurs whenever there is an imbalance between myocardial oxygen supply and demand.

[^2]: **Hypertensive urgency**: Severe hypertension (SBP ≥180mmHg, DBP ≥120mmHg) without acute end-organ damage. **Hypertensive emergency**: Severe hypertension (usually DBP ≥120mmHg) with acute end-organ damage.

## Adverse Effects

- Headache, flushing (due to vasodilation)
- Dizziness, lightheadedness
- **Peripheral edema** (ankle swelling)
- Reflex **tachycardia** (esp. short-acting forms)
- Gingival hyperplasia [^3]
- Hypotension (in overdose or rapid IV use)

[^3]: **Gingival Hyperplasia** (Overgrown Gums): is an abnormal overgrowth of gum tissue, which can cause the gums to look puffy or cover the teeth. (_Gingiv-_ = gums; _-al_ = relating to; _hyper-_ = over or above; _-plasia_ = to form or grow)

## Cautions

- Avoid short-acting `nifedipine` in hypertension → may cause **reflex sympathetic activation** & **myocardial ischemia**.
- Use cautiously in **heart failure** (can worsen edema).
- Avoid abrupt withdrawal.
- Dose adjustment in **hepatic impairment** (metabolized by CYP3A4).

## Contraindications

- **Severe hypotension**
- **Cardiogenic shock**
- **Unstable angina (early phase)**
- **Aortic stenosis** (risk of collapse from afterload drop)

## Key Points Summary

| Feature                            | Description                                                               |
| :--------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Site of action**                 | Mainly **vascular smooth muscle** (arterioles)                            |
| **Effect on heart rate**           | May **increase** (reflex tachycardia)                                     |
| **Effect on contractility**        | Minimal or mild decrease                                                  |
| **Effect on conduction (AV node)** | None                                                                      |
| **Main antihypertensive effect**   | ↓ Peripheral resistance                                                   |
| **Best used in**                   | Hypertension, angina (esp. variant), elderly, isolated systolic HTN       |
| **Short-acting risk**              | Reflex tachycardia, myocardial ischemia                                   |
| **Long-acting choice**             | `amlodipine`, `felodipine` (preferred)                                    |
| **Special uses**                   | `nimodipine` – cerebral vasospasm; `nicardipine` – hypertensive emergency |
| **Main side effects**              | Headache, flushing, **ankle edema**, tachycardia                          |
| **Avoid in**                       | Severe hypotension, aortic stenosis, unstable angina                      |
